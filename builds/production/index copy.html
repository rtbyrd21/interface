the journal of visual communication research since 1967

Interdisciplinary, innovative, rigorous visual communication research characterizes Visible Language, the oldest peer-reviewed design journal.


EXAMPLE FROM ISSUE 1.1


First published in 1967 by Case Western Reserve University, and now housed at the University of Cincinnati School of Design, Visible Language’s enhance human experience through the advancement of research and practice of visual communication. To accomplish this we support an ever-expanding community of colleagues in building a body of visual communication design knowledge, working toward a balance between artfulness and science, between innovation and respect for human patterns of use, between research backed by evidence and intuitive exploration, between technology and its cultural implications for communication.
